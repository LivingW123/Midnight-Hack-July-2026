Two markets, one primitive

Your number never touches the chain.

Both features below run on the same confidential contract primitive: a bid — or a forecast — exists on-chain only as a cryptographic commitment. Zero-knowledge proofs settle the outcome without ever opening the losing numbers.

Feature one

Prediction markets you deploy agents into

Don't take a position yourself — deploy an AI agent. Give it a name and a strategy, and it runs a research pipeline, forms a posterior, and seals an independent forecast on a real event. An oracle agent resolves it from public data.

What the chain never learns: your agent's forecast, its reasoning, and every losing prediction — sealed forever. Only the resolved outcome and the winner go public.

  • No live odds to herd on. On a transparent market the running price is the aggregate, so late traders copy it instead of forecasting. Here the aggregate only exists after everyone has committed.
  • Nobody can copy-trade your agent. Rivals see that you committed, never what.
  • Agent brains run locally — reasoning never leaves your machine.

Feature two

Sealed-bid auctions

Auctions where nobody sees your bid — not the seller, not rival bidders, not the blockchain. Bid, then reveal in zero knowledge: the contract verifies your bid opens your commitment and tracks the highest, without publishing the rest.

What the chain never learns: every losing bid amount. Only the winner's pseudonymous id and the winning price become public, because settlement needs them.

  • Five mechanisms in one contract — Dutch, uniform-price batch, candle, combinatorial, and time-locked.
  • The Dutch claim is the one to watch. The winner proves reservation ≥ current price without revealing how much more they would have paid.
  • Fraud detection that reads timing and reveal behaviour — never amounts, because there are none to read.

Built on Midnight

Everything runs on your machine — the wallet, your identity secrets, and the zero-knowledge prover. Bid amounts and nonces are Compact witnesses: private inputs that feed a local proof server and never leave this computer. The chain sees proofs, never inputs.
